Input Parameters Explained

Published: December 27, 2025

The Rig Lifecycle Simulator relies on a set of user-provided inputs to generate accurate profitability projections. Each parameter plays a specific role in modeling real-world mining conditions, from hardware specifications to economic factors. Understanding these inputs enables users to create meaningful simulations tailored to their own rigs and market outlook.

At the core are hardware-related values. Rig cost represents the initial purchase price, serving as the baseline for ROI calculations. Hash rate, measured in terahashes per second, determines the rig's computational power and directly influences revenue—the higher the hash rate, the greater the share of network rewards captured.

Operational Costs

Power consumption in watts and electricity cost per kilowatt-hour are critical for calculating monthly expenses. A typical modern ASIC miner consumes between 3000 and 4000 watts. At $0.12 per kilowatt-hour, this translates to roughly $300–$400 monthly, a fixed cost that remains constant while revenue fluctuates with difficulty.

Market and Network Factors

Coin price converts earned cryptocurrency into dollar revenue. As of late December 2025, Bitcoin trades near $88,000, significantly amplifying returns for efficient rigs. Initial difficulty reflects the current network state—around 148 trillion—and sets the starting point for reward distribution.

Projection Controls

The monthly difficulty increase percentage is one of the most impactful inputs. Users can set conservative (1–2 percent) or aggressive (5+ percent) rates based on historical trends or future expectations. The simulation period offers 24 or 36 months, aligning with typical rig operational lifecycles before major upgrades or obsolescence.

Advanced Settings

Block reward and blocks per day allow customization beyond Bitcoin defaults. The current post-2024 halving reward is 3.125 BTC, with approximately 144 blocks mined daily. Adjusting these supports modeling other proof-of-work chains or hypothetical future halvings.

Accurate inputs lead to reliable outputs. Sourcing real-time difficulty and price data ensures projections reflect current conditions, while varying the difficulty growth rate reveals sensitivity to network expansion.

FAQ

Where can I find current Bitcoin difficulty?

Sites like Blockchain.com or BTC.com provide up-to-date network statistics.

Should I include shipping or taxes in rig cost?

Yes, for the most accurate ROI, include the total upfront investment.

How does hash rate unit conversion work?

Inputs in TH/s are internally converted to hashes per second for precise reward calculations.

Experiment with different input combinations to understand your rig's potential under various market scenarios.
